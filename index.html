I am Farah Aït Salaht, an Associate Professor of Computer Science at the Pôle Universitaire Léonard de Vinci (ESILV) in Courbevoie, France, where I am a member of the De Vinci Research Center (DVRC).

I earned my Ph.D. in Computer Science in 2014 from the University of Versailles Saint-Quentin-en-Yvelines (UVSQ). My doctoral work focused on the performance evaluation of complex networks, utilizing stochastic bounds and Markov chains to solve issues related to state space explosion.

Following my PhD, I expanded my expertise through postdoctoral positions at Télécom SudParis and ENSAI, focusing on the trade-off between Quality of Service and energy consumption in Cloud systems. I later joined an Inria Discovery project dedicated to service placement in large-scale fog computing environments.

Since then, my research has focused on the Cloud-to-Edge continuum. My work primarily addresses service placement and resource management in distributed environments, with a specific emphasis on autonomous control and stochastic optimization.

Research Areas

Service Placement & Resource Management

  • Service placement and replication across the Cloud-Edge-IoT continuum
  • Latency- and energy-aware deployment strategies
  • Resource allocation in fog and edge infrastructures
  • Optimization and constraint-programming approaches

Autonomic Control & Decision-Making AI

  • Self-adaptive systems built on the MAPE-K control loop
  • Concurrent, intra-loop coordination for faster reaction
  • AI and heuristics for smart, autonomous decisions
  • Dynamic reconfiguration of service orchestration

Stochastic Modeling & QoS Guarantees

  • Performance evaluation with Markov chains and stochastic bounds
  • Quality of Service (QoS) and reliability analysis
  • Taming state-space explosion in complex networks
  • Trade-offs between QoS and energy consumption

Open Positions

PhD Position Start: October 1, 2026

PhD: Concurrent Autonomic Control for the Cloud-Edge-IoT Continuum

This PhD is part of the PEPR Cloud program (project TARANIS) and is jointly supervised by Inria (AVALON team / ENS Lyon) and ESILV – De Vinci Research Center (DVRC). The work will focus on designing, formalizing and evaluating a new MAPE-K coordination model enabling intra-loop concurrency, in order to improve the responsiveness of service orchestration systems.

Location: Paris campus, with occasional travel to Lyon
Duration: 3 years
Supervisors: Christian Perez (Inria / ENS Lyon) & Farah Aït-Salaht (DVRC / ESILV)
Candidate Profile

Engineering degree or Master 2 in Computer Science or a related field, with an interest in distributed systems, cloud/edge computing, containerized architectures, performance modeling, adaptive algorithms and decision-making AI.
